Summary

COGWAR REPORT is a research-creation project that investigates cognitive warfare through AI-powered discourse analysis and the speculative creation of digital countermeasures. The project establishes historical connections between the psychological operations of Plan Condor (1975-1983) and the cognitive warfare techniques recently formalized by NATO, analyzing how these tactics manifest in contemporary Argentine political discourse.

Through natural language processing technologies, fine-tuned AI models, and 3D semantic visualizations, the project seeks to reveal power structures operating below consciousness, while exploring speculative forms of cognitive resistance from a Global South perspective.

Introduction: Art as Immaterial Research

A large part of my artistic processes is immaterial. It looks like thinking, desiring and designing data structures, systems, datasets, processes, pipelines. They also involve studying many topics extensively because I don't think it's right to make work about things I don't "know". That's also why many of my works are related to topics I study for many years: Argentine history and revisionism, my dreams, Artificial Intelligence, programming, semiotics, etc.

Then comes a moment when I have something that for me captures something and I release it: I publish it, or present it somewhere.

Since the end of 2024 I've been researching cognitive warfare, and warfare in general. I started trying to understand how the internal theories of nations' armed forces become doctrines and strategies, and how psychology affects that process.

Context: From Deterrence to Cognitive Warfare

The Concept of Deterrence

My research began studying the concept of deterrence: deterrence is successful in a warlike context if an actor who would otherwise take action in the face of an observed conflict does not do so due to internal conviction that 1) they could not win or 2) they could win, but the consequences would be so destructive as to not justify it. In this case this actor lost without fighting, and their enemy won without lifting a finger.

This reminded me of what Jauretche said in "Manual de Zonzeras Argentinas":

"...[Pedagogical colonization] proposes impossible models and hides the possible ones..."

What interested me most about deterrence is its deeply psychological character. Your enemy has to believe that your threats are based on reality for deterrence to take effect.

The Multipolar Moment

We are at a moment where the powers are shuffling the cards and re-dealing. In Argentina, we unfortunately find ourselves at a moment of ideologized neocolonial submission. It is ideologized, because we are not offering ourselves to the strongest, but precisely to the sinking ship, due to the mystical visions of a democratically elected person.

Since I would like to be able to do something about it, I make art-technology-research.

Cognitive warfare focuses on controlling perception and labeling of reality

In that amulet there are 5 languages - English, Spanish, French, Chinese and Italian. A semantic hyperdensity that seeks to create resistance through excess meaning.

Technical Challenges as Artistic Findings

Contemporary political speeches are difficult to analyze even for native Argentines. They are full of inconsistencies, fragmented syntax, abrupt topic changes, but also density of historical and ideological references. This combination makes automatic analysis really challenging - but also reveals something about the fragmented nature of authoritarian discourse.

The Aesthetics of Surveillance

The project explores an aesthetics of suspicion, of surveillance, of sensitive paranoia. The visual modules seek to generate discomfort, awaken the intuition that "something else is happening", denaturalize the climate of political violence we live in.

Final Reflections: Art-Technology as a Field of Resistance

COGWAR REPORT is an active and ongoing project. It presents challenges that are technical as well as ethical and artistic-discursive.

Being able to relate the concept of Cognitive Warfare to Plan Condor and contemporary politics is an important task because its reality directly affects decision-making and the personal lives of inhabitants of entire countries.

Already formalized as a domain of war, cognitive protection becomes a matter of civil rights in a world where it is impossible for experts and ordinary people alike to find the distinction between peace and war.

Finding citizen forms of resilience and resistance from our cultural-technological knowledge is part of thinking about art-technology as a field of knowledge committed to the reality of society and territory.

Current Project Status (May 2025)

  • Cognitive Warfare Dataset: 29 QA pairs, 9,879 words
  • Fine-tuned model: LLaMA 3 trained on custom dataset
  • Analysis framework: Multidimensional (emotions, binarisms, geopolitics, symbolism)
  • Semantic visualizations: Operational multilingual 3D spaces
  • Interactive modules: Eye detection systems and animated ASCII amulets

Next Steps

The project aspires to become a citizen analysis tool that allows identifying and resisting cognitive warfare techniques, always maintaining commitment to technological sovereignty and democracy from the Global South.
